Project Compass

Project Compass is a community-focused initiative by Merit Network that supports Michigan communities in advancing digital equity, broadband access, and digital literacy.

Overview

Project Compass helps local communities bridge the digital divide through capacity building, educational programming, public initiatives, and strategic planning for long-term digital inclusion.

The initiative builds on the Digital Opportunities Compass (DOC) framework to assess current conditions, guide local action, and measure progress over time.

Key Objectives

  • Enhance digital skills: Teach online safety, technology basics, and practical digital literacy for services like telehealth, employment, and civic participation.
  • Strengthen community engagement: Listen to local needs, organize learning opportunities, and connect residents with resources and experts.
  • Build local capacity: Support local organizations and stakeholders in developing sustainable digital equity strategies.
  • Support infrastructure planning: Assist with broadband expansion and connectivity planning, especially in underserved and rural areas.

Framework and Approach

Project Compass uses the DOC framework's six components:

  1. Contexts
  2. Governance
  3. Connectivity
  4. Skills
  5. Application
  6. Outcomes

This structure helps communities identify priorities, implement interventions, and evaluate long-term impact. It also aligns with broader federal digital equity efforts, including the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act and the Digital Equity Act.

Funding and Impact

Project Compass has been supported by the USDA Broadband Technical Assistance Program, including a $997,000 grant to expand high-speed internet and digital equity strategies in rural Michigan communities.

The project supports survey activities, educational programs, community planning, and digital skills training at no cost to participating communities.

Participation and Collaboration

Community members, local organizations, and regional partners can participate through onboarding opportunities, local initiatives, and collaborative planning efforts.

Project Compass emphasizes collaboration across researchers, practitioners, and community leaders to create practical, community-driven plans for digital inclusion.

Project Compass takes a holistic, research-informed approach to improving digital access and literacy so Michigan communities can thrive in an increasingly digital world.
